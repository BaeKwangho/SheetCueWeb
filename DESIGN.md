# SheetCue Design System

## Product Positioning

SheetCue should feel like a precise rehearsal tool, not a generic SaaS landing
page. The page should make one idea obvious in the first viewport: a static PDF
score becomes an editable sequence of measure cues, and the user keeps control
over correction, repeats, timing, and privacy.

## Design Options Considered

1. **Quiet Apple-style launch page**
   - Strong product focus and clean typography.
   - Risk: too polished and abstract for a workflow that needs concrete trust.

2. **Score editing workstation**
   - Uses sheet-music texture, cue boxes, correction states, and step sequencing.
   - Best fit because the product is a practical editor and rehearsal aid.

3. **Privacy-first trust page**
   - Leads with local-first handling and no accounts.
   - Useful as a support theme, but too defensive as the main visual story.

The selected direction is **Score editing workstation with local-first trust**.

## Visual Principles

- Lead with the workflow, not a slogan.
- Use real score and cue imagery whenever possible.
- Make correction feel intentional: teal for confirmed cues, coral for edits,
  blue for playback.
- Keep surfaces crisp and utilitarian: 8px radii, fine borders, restrained
  shadows, dense but readable sections.
- Avoid decorative gradients, floating blobs, and generic SaaS card stacks.

## Palette

- Ink: `#171513`
- Muted ink: `#5f5a52`
- Paper: `#fbfaf7`
- Staff line: `#ded8ce`
- Cue teal: `#16b8a6`
- Correction coral: `#e15f4f`
- Playback blue: `#2f6fbd`
- Deep panel: `#101614`

## Typography

- Headings use Manrope for clear product confidence.
- Body uses Source Sans 3 for readable technical copy.
- Headlines should be compact and literal. Avoid marketing exaggeration.

## Layout

- Hero: centered copy, visible workflow rail, product image below, no split hero.
- Proof strip: three compact claims with icons and strong labels.
- Benefits: alternating text and product image, with tool-like bullets.
- Workflow: four numbered steps that read as a sequencer.
- Privacy: quiet trust panel, not a fear-based section.
- CTA: release status and policy links with clear next actions.

## Motion

Motion should be subtle and utilitarian. Fade/slide section reveals are fine;
large bouncy movement should be avoided because the page is about precision.
